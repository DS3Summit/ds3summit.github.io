Panel on Software Fuzzing

Featuring:
Marcel Böhme is a 2019 ARC DECRA Fellow and Senior Lecturer (A/Prof) at Monash University, Australia. Marcel’s research is focussed on the theoretical foundations and the practical tooling around software security, where he investigates practical aspects of automated vulnerability discovery, such as efficiency, scalability, and assurances. Marcel leads his group with a reproducibility policy. All tools and data are made available as open-source, and in some cases have been upstreamed for integration into popular fuzzers, such as AFL++ and LibFuzzer.

Justin Campbell is a Principal Group Engineering Manager at Microsoft focused on Windows and Microsoft platform security including vulnerability testing at scale, sandboxing, and mitigations. Most recently, he has been involved with Project OneFuzz, an extensible fuzz testing framework available through GitHub as an open-source tool, making the testing framework used by Microsoft Edge, Windows, and teams across Microsoft available to developers around the world. Prior to Microsoft, Justin spent nearly 15 years at a variety of contractors focused on offensive security.

Konstantin (Kostya) Serebryany is a Software Engineer at Google. His team develops and deploys dynamic testing tools, such as AddressSanitizer, MemorySanitizer, ThreadSanitizer, and libFuzzer. Prior to joining Google in 2007, Konstantin spent 4 years at Elbrus/MCST working for Sun compiler lab and then 3 years at Intel Compiler Lab. Konstantin holds a PhD from mesi.ru and a M.S. from msu.ru.

Moderator: Mathias Payer
